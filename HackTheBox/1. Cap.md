## Reports

This report presents the results of a vulnerability assessment conducted to identify any vulnerabilities, which then we found there are three vulnerabilities identified. Those said vulnerability level ranged from high to critical due to misconfiguration and negligence of an employee. We found an IDOR level of vulnerability that allows access to admin/root, and then an unencrypted password that we can download through the website, along with a misconfiguration of sudo command.

#### Overall Risk Posture

Based on the assessment results, the organization’s overall security posture is assessed as High risk, primarily driven by identified weaknesses that could allow unauthorized privilege elevation.

#### Business Impact Overview
Successful exploitation of identified vulnerabilities could result in unauthorized administrative access, compromise of sensitive systems, disruption of services, and potential regulatory or compliance exposure.

### Mitigation Recommendation

#### Recommended Remediation
 Reconfigure for the IDOR, adding access control check and avoid exposing id in URL and POST bodies. Always use UUIDs as primary keys. Do not give access of .pcap through the download button, instead use a verification before being able to download the file. Always update the outdated application to prevent a misconfigured product.
Reconfigure the setcap of python 3.8

#### Compensating Controls
 No compensating controls required; vulnerability remediation is feasible.

 ☑ Immediate ☐ Short-term ☐ Long-term

## Lesson Learned

Most of the time, we forgot to update our privilege access. We oftenly keep the insecure object and login through insecure FTP port. As a cybersecurity analyst, it is a must to make sure the workflow and business logic of a web app safe. In this case study, we also learned that a misconfiguration of python3 can leads us to a disaster, of which giving an access to Root without inputing any password.
